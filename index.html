Orthopedic
Rehabilitation

Orthopedic rehabilitation is a crucial aspect of recovery for individuals who have experienced musculoskeletal injuries, surgeries, or chronic conditions affecting bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, and muscles. It focuses on restoring mobility, strength, and function through targeted exercises and interventions.

  • Read More

Orthopedic injuries we can assist with:

  • Fractures (e.g., broken bones in the arm, leg, or spine)
  • Sprains (e.g., ankle sprain)
  • Strains (e.g., pulled muscle or tendon, such as in the back or hamstring)
  • Tendonitis (e.g., inflammation of the tendon, often in the shoulder or elbow)
  • Ligament injuries (e.g., ACL tears in the knee)
  • Dislocations (e.g., shoulder or finger dislocation)
  • Osteoarthritis (e.g., degeneration of joint cartilage, commonly in the knees, hips, or hands)
  • Rotator cuff injuries (e.g., shoulder tendon or muscle injuries)
  • Herniated discs (e.g., slipped discs in the spine causing back pain)
  • Carpal Tunnel Syndrome (e.g., compression of the median nerve in the wrist)
  • Bursitis (e.g., inflammation of the fluid-filled sacs that cushion joints)

Chronic Disease
Management

Chronic Disease Management involves the ongoing support and treatment of individuals with long-term chronic illnesses. At CB Bio, we create personalised exercise and rehabilitation programs to help manage symptoms, improve quality of life, and prevent complications.

  • Read More

Chronic Diseases we can help manage:

  • Fractures (e.g., broken bones in the arm, leg, or spine)
  • Diabetes (Type 1 and Type 2): Using exercise to improve blood sugar control, manage weight, and enhance cardiovascular health.
  • Hypertension: Helping to lower blood pressure through safe, regular physical activity and lifestyle changes.
  • ⁠Stroke Recovery: Enhancing mobility and coordination post-stroke through tailored rehabilitation exercises.
  • Osteoarthritis: Managing joint pain and stiffness by improving flexibility, strength, and function in affected joints.
  • Cardiovascular Disease: Enhancing heart function, endurance, and Chronic Pain Conditions (e.g., fibromyalgia, chronic low back pain): Alleviating pain and improving movement through low-impact exercises and strengthening programs.
  • Obesity: Aiding in weight management and reducing associated risks (such as joint stress and diabetes) through tailored exercise regimens.
  • Parkinson’s Disease: Improving movement, balance, and flexibility to manage symptoms and slow disease progression.

Neurological
Rehabilitation

Neurological Rehabilitation focuses on improving movement, coordination, and functional independence in individuals affected by disorders or injuries of the nervous system.

  • Read More

Through guided, progressive exercise therapy, we aim to enhance mobility, balance, strength, and overall quality of life. Our programs also help retrain the nervous system, improve posture and gait, and support daily functioning, empowering clients to regain confidence and independence in their movements.
Neurological disease our practice can assist with:

  • Stroke Recovery
    • Rebuilding strength, coordination, and balance
    • Improving gait and mobility through task-specific exercises
  • Parkinson’s Disease
    • Enhancing motor control, posture, and balance
    • Managing tremors and improving movement fluidity
  • Multiple Sclerosis (MS)
    • Promoting stability, strength, and energy conservation
    • Reducing fatigue and maintaining functional independence
  • Peripheral Neuropathy
    • Improving proprioception, balance, and walking ability
    • Reducing fall risk through stability training
